Bitcoin is at a crossroads. Just a few major currency exchanges or "cambios" serve the majority of the Bitcoin economy. With such a significant majority of Bitcoin transactions operating through so few cambios, the entire Bitcoin economy is at risk. From over-regulation or with just a few exchanges failing, the Bitcoin economy could collapse as a result. We propose the development of a peer-to-peer value exchange platform. This platform will scale Bitcoin by allowing parties to directly transact with one another in a private and secure fashion. The reliance on a select few cambios can be eliminated. Thus, a much more robust Bitcoin economy can develop and flourish.

FAQ

Why do we need such a platform?
Currently, Bitcoin does not have a virtual currency exchange mechanism. Parties must use individual currency and value exchanges such as Mt. Gox. Unfortunately, the existing exchange model is vulnerable to over regulation or systemic failure. A "virtual" cambio must be developed. Cambix proposes provide system in which privacy of transactions can maintained while insuring that attackers may not subvert the online system.
What are the requirements for the platform?
The Internet currently lacks a ubiquitous mechanism by which parties can discover what other people wish to sell using Bitcoin and provide a verfiable mechanism for them to exchange.
What other components are needed to make the platform succesful?
The platform requires a secure, discoverable mechanism in which parties can communicate with one another without others interferring with that communication (such as "spam" or false transactions). There several other open source projects which can assist this one. Specifically, BitMessage ("PyBitmessage"), ensures that parties receive cryptographically secure messages over a broadcast network and have spam prevention features.In addition, Open-Transactions will allow for parties to transact business anonymously and verifiably through the network. By combining this facilities with a virtual exchange, users will be able to interact and transact business for virtual, financial and physical goods.
Isn't OpenCoin's Ripple currency (XRP) going to solve this problem?
First and foremost, OpenCoin's Ripple currency is not truly "open." It is a proprietary system designed to establish Ripples as the primary medium of exchange amongst the parties. (See:"Ripple Scam" .) Ripples are "pre-mined" property of OpenCoin. As such, the approach neither fits with the philosophy or "fairness" of Bitcoin. Hence, a virtual exchange platform that fits in with the spirit of Bitcoin must be developed. Cambix proposes a system in which Bitcoin, itself, can be used as that medium of exchange.
